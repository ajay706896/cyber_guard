Why Strong Passwords Matter

In 2024, a weak password is an open invitation. Brute-force attacks, where attackers use automated software to guess millions of passwords per second, are more common than ever. A password like "123456" can be cracked instantly.

A strong, complex password increases the "entropy," or randomness, making it exponentially harder for these tools to guess. A 12-character password with letters, numbers, and symbols could take centuries to crack, while an 8-character password with only letters could take just hours.

This tool helps you visualize that strength and build a better digital fortress.

How Our Scoring Works

We don't just check boxes. Our algorithm calculates a "strength score" based on multiple factors to give you a realistic assessment:

  • Length (Weight: 40%): The most critical factor. We award significant points for every character, especially after 8 and 12.
  • Character Variety (Weight: 60%): We check for four distinct types of characters:
    • Uppercase Letters (A-Z)
    • Lowercase Letters (a-z)
    • Numbers (0-9)
    • Special Symbols (!@#$%^&*)

The score is a combination of these elements, capped at 100. A "Very Strong" password (90+) has high length and all four character types, making it highly resistant to modern attacks.
